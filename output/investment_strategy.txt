The investment strategy involves diversifying the portfolio across different asset classes, including stocks, bonds, and cash. The target asset allocation is 60% stocks, 30% bonds, and 10% cash. This allocation is based on historical data and is intended to provide a balance between risk and potential returns. The portfolio will be monitored and adjusted regularly to ensure it remains aligned with the investment goals and risk tolerance. Additionally, the strategy includes risk management techniques such as stop-loss orders and diversification, as well as tax-efficient investing strategies like tax-loss harvesting. Dollar-cost averaging will be used to reduce the impact of market volatility on the portfolio.
